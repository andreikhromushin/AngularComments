On being briefly here

Andrei Khromushin · 1 min read

The universe is older than imagination and almost entirely empty. Billions of stars, billions of years, billions of accidents had to line up exactly right for any of us to wake up on a Tuesday and complain about the weather. Somewhere out there, in another galaxy, something is probably doing the same — and we'll almost certainly never meet. The strangest thing about being briefly alive on a small blue planet isn't that it ends. It's that it ever started at all. That seems like a reason to enjoy it.
